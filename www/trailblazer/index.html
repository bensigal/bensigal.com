Purchase

Everyone gets to bid as much as they want. This is in the format "3 at 4," which means 3 are being bought for $4 each. Once everyone is done bidding, goods are bought according to the bids, in order from highest unit price to lowest. Tie goes to whoever said it first. If there are not enough to be purchased, buy as many as possible.

Movement

Roll off to see who goes first. Highest roller moves all their fleets, then player to their right, etc. Determine distance from fleet to destination. Moving to an adjacent space is 2 points, a "diagonal" one is 3. Remember this number. Roll a number of dice: 2 for a fleet of transports, 3 for mixed or all scouts. Exploration

Exploration is not included in an Introductory game.

Selling Goods

Later demand letters mean that number sold vs. demand number matters more. For example, say a world demands with a number of 8, and 3 units are being sold. A demand letter of "A" would get you the least money, because the lack of supply doesn't matter as much. To contrast, if 10 units were being sold, a demand letter of "F" would get you the least money, as the oversupply matters much more.

Maintenance

Each player must spend $5 per ship, factor, and home office, or it is destroyed. You may also buy factors during this phase for $10 each. You do not have to pay maintenance for a factor on the turn it is created. After this, calculate Sales Tax. This is 10% of the money you made selling on Sol only, rounded up.

End of Game

Before the game starts, the player agree on the number of game turns to be completed. Once the final turn is over, everybody gains $10 oer ship, $10 per factor or Home Office, and $2 per unsold unit of goods. The player with the most points wins. Ties are not broken.
